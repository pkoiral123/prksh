May 8, 2024

Investigation of effects of thermal barrier coatings on gasoline compression ignition combustion

This is the project of my MS Thesis. A coupled modeling of CFD and FEA to simultaneously analyze fluid flow and structural behaviors under various conditions was used until a quasi-steady state was achieved. A coating material called Gadolinium Zirconate (Gd-Zr) was used for TBC on the piston of a GCI engine running at 1500rpm and at three different operating conditions of 6-bar, 10-bar, and 15-bar. The goal was to use modeling to determine how this coating affects the engine's performance. We found that the TBC made the engine's surface temperature swing more than 100K, and this helped reduce heat loss from the gas to the walls, making the engine more efficient.

November 15, 2023

Analysis of soot emissions using phenomenological soot modeling for gasoline-ethanol blend ratios in GCI combustion: A CFD Simulation Study

The effect of start of injection timing, and fuel composition on the soot emissions for gasoline ethanol blend ratios of E10 and E50 under ten bar conditions were studied. The amount of soot mass was found to be lower for E50 comparatively due to higher ethanol content. The soot emissions was found to be maximum for -31 CAD SOI1 conditions and followed the trend SOI -31 > SOI -25 > SOI -19 CAD.

Design, Modeling and Simulation of Series Hybrid Electric Vehicle (HEV)

A series HEV was modeled and simulated in Simulink in reference to US06 drive cycle. September 21, 2021

Design, and Analysis of Closed Circuit Low Speed Subsonic Wind Tunnel

A complete design methodology for a closed circuit subsonic wind tunnel with an axial fan and flow analysis using CFD simulations (ANSYS Fluent) for the designed wind tunnel was performed. Wind tunnel with test section of 2.60 × 1.84 m and maximum test section velocity of 100 m/s was designed. Using analytical methods, overall dimensions and loss coefficients for each component was calculated that yield Energy ratio of 6.9. For this obtained Energy ratio, a single stage axial fan was designed by implementing the isolated airfoil theory for blade design and free vortex flow for three-dimensional flow equilibrium.

November 14, 2020

Design, Analysis and Evaluation of Electric All Terrain Vehicle (E-ATV)

The vehicle frame as well as all the systems are designed, modeled and analyzed using SolidWorks, ANSYS, MATLAB and Simulink. Two different roll cage models were designed and a crash testing was performed where a suitable model was selected from the analysis. The overall tractive power requirement to run EATV at an average velocity of 25 km/hr on 25% grade was found to be 10.57 kW, assuming the range to be 40 km in one full charge. Double wishbones system was selected for suspension whereas disc brakes was selected for braking system. Similarly, rack and pinion steering system was designed and used rather than power steering system.
