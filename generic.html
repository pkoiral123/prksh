April 25, 2024

Graduate Research Assistant

    I have worked as an active member of Advanced Combustion and Renewable Fuels (ACRF) Laboratory for about two year period. I have performed my research works on computational models and methods as applied to internal combustion engines where I used CONVERGE, ANSYS Fluent, ABAQUS, SolidWorks, MATLAB and Tecplot as main tools. Additonally, I performed all my simulations on Palmetto High Performance Computing (HPC) Cluster.

  • View Lab Website

As a member of Clemson ACRF Lab, I spend my time on several projects utilizing the modeling tools like CFD and FEA. I worked on thermal barrier coatings (TBCs) on advanced low temperature gasoline compression ignition (LT-GCI) combustion mode and also on coventional diesel combustion. In this advanced combustion mode, the modeling predicted an efficiency gain of about 0.5 percentage points by minimizing the heat transfer losses across the piston walls which is due to temperature swing phenomenon of TBCs. I, along with my modeling team developed a CFD-FEA Co-simulation framework where the near wall bondary conditions from CFD were fed into FEA so that a more accurate prediction of piston surface temperature swing could be done which is essential for TBCs. In order to input the boudary conditions to FEA, we developed a MATLAB script that computes near wall gas temperature and heat transfer coefficient at each crank angle during the engine cycle.



June 14, 2022

Design Engineer

    I worked as an Engine design and modeling engineer at Nepal Academy of Science and Technology (NAST) Laboratory for about six months duration since January 2022. I worked along with three other members in the project regarding plastic waste and used cooking oil as an alternative fuel in IC engine. All the blends of biodiesel taken under our research shows engine performance as good as pure diesel such as indicated power, brake power, indicated thermal efficiency, brake thermal efficiency etc. The CFD modeling was performed in ANSYS Fluent where the model was validated against the experimental results. All the engine components were designed and modeled in SolidWorks followed by simulation on Fluent. A biodiesel blend of 15%-25% could be used as alternative fuel source in IC engine but to be specific; blend of 25% biodiesel is best suited and much more useful as maximum of diesel could be replaced. The modeling results agreed with experiment predicting a blend of 25% biodiesel as best suited and much more useful as maximum of diesel could be replaced. All the samples used for emission test were better than pure diesel. B20 sample resulted to an extraordinary emission performance while using in ford diesel engine. Thus, blends of high-grade plastic oil with diesel up to 20% by volume and blends of low-grade plastic oil diesel up to 10% by volume show a high cetane index of the fuel which improves the combustion characteristics of the engine.
