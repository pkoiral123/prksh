April 25, 2024

Graduate Research Assistant

I have worked as an active member of Advanced Combustion and Renewable Fuels (ACRF) Laboratory for about two year period. I have performed my research works on computational models and methods as applied to internal combustion engines where I used CONVERGE, ANSYS Fluent, ABAQUS, SolidWorks, MATLAB and Tecplot as main tools. Additonally, I performed all my simulations on Palmetto High Performance Computing (HPC) Cluster.

  • View Lab Website

As a member of Clemson ACRF Lab, I spend my time on several projects utilizing the modeling tools like CFD and FEA. I worked on thermal barrier coatings (TBCs) on advanced low temperature gasoline compression ignition (LT-GCI) combustion mode and also on coventional diesel combustion. In this advanced combustion mode, the modeling predicted an efficiency gain of about 0.5 percentage points by minimizing the heat transfer losses across the piston walls which is due to temperature swing phenomenon of TBCs. I, along with my modeling team developed a CFD-FEA Co-simulation framework where the near wall bondary conditions from CFD were fed into FEA so that a more accurate prediction of piston surface temperature swing could be done which is essential for TBCs. In order to input the boudary conditions to FEA, we developed a MATLAB script that computes near wall gas temperature and heat transfer coefficient at each crank angle during the engine cycle.
